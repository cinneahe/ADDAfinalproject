Blake Bolden

“Blake Bolden” chronicles the life of Blake Bolden, a Boston College alumna and professional women's ice hockey player for the Boston Blades, a team in the elite Canadian Women's Hockey League. Blake is not your average professional ice hockey player: she juggles a 9-5 job at a non-profit, hits the ice nightly for practices, and competes against other teams in the league on weekends. This film both highlights Blake as a Black woman breaking barriers in a sport lacking diversity as well as the challenges facing women in professional sports.

A Cleveland native, Blake Bolden (23) enjoyed an illustrious career at Boston College including an appearance at the Frozen Four and distinctions such as Hockey East Defenseman of the Year and First Team Hockey East All Star. In 2013, the Boston Blades of the Canadian Women’s Hockey League (CWHL) drafted her, making her the first African-American ever to be drafted in the first round. She helped lead the Boston Blades to a Clarkson Cup victory. She was also named a CWHL 2014-2015 All-Star.
